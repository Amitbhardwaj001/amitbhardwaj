What is Soil Erosion?

Soil erosion is the process by which soil is removed or displaced from its original location, often by natural forces like wind and water, or human activities such as deforestation and construction.

Causes of Soil Erosion

  • Deforestation
  • Overgrazing
  • Improper agricultural practices
  • Urban development

Effects of Soil Erosion

Soil erosion can lead to:

  • Loss of fertile topsoil
  • Reduced agricultural productivity
  • Increased sedimentation in rivers and lakes
  • Environmental degradation

Preventing Soil Erosion

To prevent soil erosion, measures like planting cover crops, contour farming, and maintaining natural vegetation can be adopted.
